PLUCK

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 23 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Pluck ("Pluck", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you use the Pluck mobile application and related services (the "Service"). Pluck is a conversation-practice app that lets you rehearse real-world conversations (such as interviews, presentations, and difficult discussions) with AI-driven characters and receive feedback on your performance.

For the purposes of the UK GDPR and EU GDPR, the data controller is the individual developer who operates Pluck. If you have any questions or wish to exercise your rights, contact us at ak@pluckapp.net.

We designed Pluck to collect as little personal data as possible. You can use most of the app as a guest, without an account. We do not sell your personal data, and we do not show third-party advertising.

1. Information we collect

Information you provide

  • Account information — if you create an account, your email address and a securely hashed version of your password. If you use Sign in with Apple, we receive an Apple identifier and, if you choose to share it, your email address.
  • Profile information — an optional display name, chosen avatar, and in-app preferences.
  • Conversation content — the messages you type or speak during a practice session, which are processed to generate the other character's replies and your feedback.
  • Communications — if you email us for support, the contents of that correspondence.

Information collected automatically

  • Usage and progress data — your practice history, scores, streaks, levels, and in-app activity, used to power the journey, leagues, and progress features.
  • A device identifier — a random identifier generated on your device, used to detect sign-ins from new devices and to keep your progress in sync. It is not a government or hardware identifier.
  • Technical data — limited information such as IP address and request metadata processed by our hosting provider to deliver and secure the Service.

3. AI processing of your conversations

To generate the other character's responses and your feedback, the text of your practice messages is sent to our AI provider, Anthropic, which processes it on our behalf to return a result. We do not use your conversation content to advertise to you, and we instruct our provider not to use it to train their models on our behalf where such controls are available.

AI feedback is automated and for practice purposes only. It may be inaccurate or incomplete and is not professional, medical, psychological, therapeutic, legal, or career advice. You should not rely on it as a substitute for advice from a qualified professional.

We may also disclose data where required by law, to enforce our Terms, or to protect the rights, safety, and property of our users or others. We do not sell your personal data.

5. International data transfers

Some of our providers process data outside the UK/EEA, including in the United States. Where this happens, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the UK/EU adequacy mechanisms (including the UK–US Data Bridge / EU–US Data Privacy Framework where the recipient is certified) and/or Standard Contractual Clauses with the UK Addendum.

6. How long we keep your data

We keep your account and progress data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, we delete your account data from our live systems promptly (see Section 8). We may retain limited records where required for legal, security, or accounting purposes, and backups are overwritten on a rolling basis.

7. Security

Passwords are stored only as salted, hashed values — never in plain text. Data in transit is encrypted (HTTPS), and access to our systems is restricted. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we take reasonable measures to protect your data.

8. Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to: access your data; correct inaccurate data; delete your data; restrict or object to processing; data portability; and withdraw consent at any time. You can:

  • Delete your account and data directly in the app — open your account settings and choose "Delete account". This permanently removes your account and associated data from our servers.
  • Contact us at ak@pluckapp.net to exercise any other right. We aim to respond within one month.

If you are in the UK or EEA and believe we have not handled your data properly, you may complain to your local supervisory authority — in the UK, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

9. Children

Pluck is intended for users aged 13 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal data, contact us and we will delete it.

10. Cookies and tracking

The app stores data locally on your device (for example, your preferences and a sign-in token) to make the Service work. We do not use third-party advertising or cross-app tracking.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will update the "Last updated" date above and, where changes are significant, notify you in the app or by email.

12. Contact

Questions or requests: ak@pluckapp.net.
